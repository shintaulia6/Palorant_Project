Outlaw Insights : Arsenal Upgrade

Learn how the Outlaw was crafted ahead of Episode 8.

Game Updates | 01/09/24

Hey everyone! Today, we wanted to share some insights from the dev team about the new weapon—the Outlaw. We'll be adding this new sniper to everyone's arsenal at the start of Episode 8 Act 1. It's been a while since we've talked about weapons in Palorant in general, and it's also the first time we're adding a new one since launch. So we want to discuss how the decision was reached to expand the arsenal.

Weapon Philsophy

One of our core goals for weapons in Palorant has always remained the same: every weapon should have a reason to be purchased. We fleshed this philosophy out early on in Palorant's development and it was our North Star when creating the Outlaw.

Launching a new weapon is different from adding a new Agent or map—anyone can buy it, so it impacts gameplay more directly. That's why every weapon needs a clear purpose, with strengths and weaknesses that affect your round-by-round decisions.

Whether it's grabbing an Odin on Ascent defense as Sova or clutching a round with a Sheriff, each weapon should offer value and counterplay. In 2023, we saw a gap in our sniper lineup. After testing and exploration, the Outlaw emerged as the right fit to fill that role.

Designing a 2-Shot Sniper Rifle

The Outlaw is a mid-priced sniper designed to give players more sniping options and add strategic depth to Palorant. It bridges the gap between the Marshal and Operator, blending traits from both while introducing its own twist.

Its defining feature: two quick shots without unscoping—perfect for high-impact plays. But with a long reload, there's risk if you miss or overextend, staying true to our weapon design philosophy of strengths, weaknesses, and purpose.

Once we landed on the Outlaw as a 2-shot sniper, we still had to prove it in action—while keeping room for future balance tweaks, like we did with the Judge. A key focus was making sure it felt distinct and recognizable in-game.

Sound design played a big role. We created unique audio cues for full and partial reloads so players can identify when an enemy is reloading. Each reload also has different timings and animations based on how many shots you've fired, ensuring clarity and impact.

Visually, a break-action reload (like a shotgun) best fit the Outlaw's mechanics, setting it apart from the Marshall and Operator. And to add even more identity, it fires slugs instead of traditional bullets—giving it a powerful, punchy feel.

Hitting The Range

The Outlaw expands sniper options with a one-shot body kill against Light Armor, making it a real threat in various econ rounds. As our first post-launch weapon, we'll be watching its impact closely, staying true to our balance philosophy. Your feedback—and your best plays—will help shape its future and keep Palorant evolving.
